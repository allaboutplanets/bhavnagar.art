Brief description about the
art Category

The 1940’s were an important chapter for art in India. This was a time when the very fabric of India was changing drastically. The creatives of the nation, the artists, writers and poets were weaving these stories into their body of work.
The city of Bombay was already becoming a hub for the artists with the formation of the Bombay Art Society in the late 1800’s. The then Maharaja of Bhavnagar, Takhatsinhji was one of the patrons of the arts. As Bombay built its reputation of a creative and artistic centre, six modern artists came together to create the Progressive Artists’ Group (PAG) in 1947.
FN Souza, KH Ara, SH Raza, MF Husain, SK Bakre and HA Gade formed this group breaking away from the Bengal revivalists.
They brought together Indian artistic elements, European movements and techniques and infused their own experiences and struggles to create masterpieces that today narrate an integral chapter of Indian art history. These works of art have also gone on to becoming collectors items.
The following category aims to inspire you as an artist to create your own style, drawing influence from the six masters of the Progressive Artists’ Group.
